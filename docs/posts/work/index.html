NMRKT

Marketing page design and development.

During the Techstars in Austin program I worked with NMRKT on a variety of different projects. Most notably, I redesigned and built their landing page. The new design has seen an increase in conversions and help communicate exactly what NMRKT stands for to potential customers, clients and investors. I designed the experience first, then moved into Photoshop for intricate details and later coded the site using Foundation and Sass.

Fashion Metric

Consumer marketing site design and development.

The team at Fashion Metric needed a new way to communicate their consumer facing site, Threadhound. I worked with the team to design and build a site that would encourage signups and communicate a sleek style matching the rest of their brand. I designed and built the site with Bootstrap using LESS.

#tsmeterohack

Meteor.js Application.

The Techstars program is known for pushing its participants beyond what they normally would think is possible. As a Hackstar with the program I worked with the teams to help with design and development in lots of ways. Many times, it was required to to learn new skills all together to complete a project. In that light, a few various team members and I met one Saturday and organized a hackathon around the JavaScript framework Meteor. I loosely followed a tutorial online and built a small app collecting all the participants at the hackathon. Designed with Bootstrap and hosted with Meteor hosting I learned how to build my first full stack single-page application.

Cloud66

Web App design and development

Cloud 66 is the best way to manage and deploy Ruby application to any stack. A SaaS product geared towards developers of all shapes and sizes need to be well supported by documentation for every nook and cranny of the application. Cloud 66 and I worked together to build out a community feature and presentation of their support docs. I worked directly with Cloud 66 CEO, Khash Sajadi, to build a a front end for a Rails application that will help drive their users to a better overall experience with Cloud 66.
